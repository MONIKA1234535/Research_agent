News Report: AI Screening Tools Reshape Entry-Level Hiring in 2026

A growing share of companies are adopting AI-based resume screening and interview
tools for entry-level technical hiring, according to a survey of 400 mid-sized
tech firms conducted earlier this year. Roughly 62% of respondents reported using
some form of AI screening in their hiring pipeline, up from 38% two years earlier.

Recruiters cited faster turnaround times as the primary driver, with average
time-to-first-response for candidates dropping from nine days to under three days
at companies using automated screening. However, several hiring managers expressed
concern that keyword-matching systems undervalue candidates from non-traditional
backgrounds whose resumes don't closely mirror standard job description language.

Candidate Experience
A separate survey of recent graduates found mixed reactions to AI-conducted
interviews. Some candidates appreciated the reduced scheduling friction, while
others reported feeling that AI interviewers failed to probe follow-up questions
in the same way a human interviewer would, particularly around project depth and
genuine understanding of submitted work.

Industry Response
Several companies have begun requiring candidates to explain their submitted code
or projects live, specifically to verify hands-on understanding rather than
AI-assisted output alone. This trend has been particularly notable in AI and
machine learning roles, where companies want assurance that candidates can reason
about model behavior rather than simply having used an AI coding assistant to
generate a working demo.

Outlook
Analysts expect AI screening adoption to continue rising through 2027, alongside
growing emphasis on live technical verification steps designed to confirm genuine
candidate skill. Several firms interviewed said they plan to combine automated
screening with mandatory live walkthroughs of any AI-assisted project work before
extending offers.
