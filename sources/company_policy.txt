Company AI Usage Policy

This document outlines how employees may use AI tools within the organization.

Approved Tools
Employees may use approved AI assistants including Claude, ChatGPT, and Groq-hosted
open models for drafting documents, writing code, and summarizing meetings. Use of
unapproved AI tools requires written sign-off from the IT security team.

Data Handling
No customer personal data, financial records, or unreleased product information may
be pasted into any external AI tool. Employees must use the internal redacted-data
sandbox for any task involving sensitive information. Violations are reported to the
Data Protection Officer within 24 hours of discovery.

Code Generated by AI
All AI-generated code must be reviewed and understood by the submitting engineer
before merge. Reviewers may reject a pull request if the author cannot explain the
logic of AI-generated sections during code review. This policy exists to prevent
silent bugs and maintain team-wide code comprehension.

Model Usage Limits
Each employee has a monthly token budget of 2 million tokens across approved tools.
Requests beyond this budget require manager approval. Engineering teams working on
active incidents are exempt from this limit for the duration of the incident.

Review Cycle
This policy is reviewed every six months by the AI Governance Committee, consisting
of one representative each from Engineering, Legal, and HR. The next scheduled
review is in January 2027. Employees may submit feedback through the internal
policy portal at any time.

Disciplinary Action
First-time policy violations result in a documented warning and mandatory retraining.
Repeated violations, particularly involving sensitive data exposure, may result in
termination in line with the company's standard disciplinary procedure.
